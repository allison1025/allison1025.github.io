storyteller + creator

hello, nice to meet you!!

I’m Allison Yih: a San Francisco-based data scientist and vegan foodie. I’m passionate about using analytical technologies to extract insights that answer society’s lingering questions.

Before jumping into the world of big data, I studied viola for a decade. During this time, I’ve realized each piece has a story that must be woven together. To achieve that, I began to deeply interpret the relationships between the notes and the musical symbols to craft the story they tell together.

Through my music background, I’ve learned to value the power of storytelling in my analytics career. Like each measure of music, I must synthesize the data with my qualitative research to effectively communicate cohesive insights behind the data, which others can utilize to make well-informed decisions.

Outside of work, you can find me

  • blogging about my worldwide cafe-hopping adventures in search of the best matcha latte

  • geeking out on productivity and minimalism articles + podcasts

  • hiking at golden hour

As someone with diverse interests, from the biology behind polyphasic sleep cycles to social media analytics, I constantly strive to learn new things everyday and expand my technical skillset. Feel free to connect with me if you'd like to chat!

some things I've created

At-Risk Communitities for COVID-19 Identifier

A Machine Learning model that simulates the effectiveness of intervention programs targeting COVID-19's risk indicators, in order to recommend measures that'd decrease neighborhood disease vulnerability

CyPlot

A protein data visualization application that integrates Plotly.js graphing APIs, allowing users to view and save their plots online

PolyLayout

A plugin that displays a molecular interaction dataset's unique categories as an interactive and uniquely structured polygon

Grand Canyon Visitor Forecast

An univariate time series model that predicts the number of national park visitors with 95% accuracy, which provides park administrators with detailed information about visitor trends that'd help them forecast spending and regulate park resources
